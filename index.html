Project ZipZap

Description

Project ZipZap is a single-player, isometric, movement focused video game where the player uses different dash abilities to fight groups of enemies, and to traverse the environment, with the ability to chain different dashes together to cause unique interactions.

Details

This game is made as a university project with 10 other people, collaborating together as a studio to create a full, ready to release game within the time span of one year.

We decided to split the task by their aspects from the game, and assign each person to an aspect of the game. This allows each of us to fully understand an aspect of the game and reduce the time of trying to understand someone else's work for the task assigned to us.

Role - Enemies

In this project, I mainly took the lead on designing and implementing the enemy encounters. This included:

Enemy Design

- Created variety of enemy types, each with unique behaviours.
- Defined enemy stats and abilities, while ensuring a balanced and engaging challenge for players.
- Managed and made sure that all enemies, both in visual and technical design fits within the theme of the game.

Arena Design

- Created an arena template that can easily be used to turn parts of the levels designed by the level design team to a working arena.
- Placed enemies and consumables strategically within levels to create a sense of progression and challenge.
- Considered factors like level flow, player visibility, and cover when designing enemy placements.

System Implementation

- Designed the AI of each enemy that adapts with changes of conditions in the arena. Giving the enemies interaction between each other.

Impact & Achievements

Some accomplishment I've achieved with the systems I've implemented according to our playtests:
- Increased player engagement by balancing arenas, keeping game flow, and controlling arena difficulty level.
- Improved player's retention by creating a satisfying challenge curve. Shown by player's willingness to keep trying when they failed a level multiple times.
- Created enemy types that are memorable and easily distinguishable from each other.


Laze

Description

Laze is a 2D reverse bullet hell game where the player gets punished by doing actions, forcing them to strategise their actions and behaviours differently.

Details

This project is in part of my dissertation project in university, of proposing a way to help jump start the development process of enemy AIs in Unity. The project includes a template of a base enemy prefab, a behaviour tree to help visualise the AI's behaviour flow, and object pools to help reduce the load of spawning and despawning enemies.

Enemies

Examples of enemies made with the system:

- Normal Melee Enemy
Only chasing and attacking player with melee attacks, these enemies serves as the most basic enemy type.
- Ranged Enemy
Attacks with a shooting projectiles, this enemy type compensates for the normal enemies' lack of range.
- Shielded Enemy
With a shield that tracks the player's position and rotates around them, the shielded enemy protects other enemies by standing between them. If their shield is broken however, they will chase the player like the normal enemies.
Bomber Enemy
Shooting exploding projectiles, the bomber enemy compensates for other enemies' lack of AOE damage. If they ran out of bullets or gets defeated, they explode to multiple exploding projectiles.

Enemy Interactions

To make enemies' behaviour less predictable, interactions between enemies are made. The interactions are as follows:

Ranged & Shielded Enemy Interactions
Shielded enemies will stay within a distance away from the player. Ranged enemies will look for a shielded enemy that still has their shield, and stands behind them, hiding from the player. If the shielded enemy is defeated, the ranged enemy will look for another shielded enemy.
Bomber Enemy Interaction
As bomber enemy's projectile can damage other enemies, all enemies will try to avoid the projectiles. If an exploding projectile gets too close to an enemy, the enemy will calculate a path away from the projectiles.

Messy Theater

Description

Messy Theater is a local multiplayer game where players have to make a king laugh by interacting with each other or objects in the scene.

Details

This game was made with a 4-person group for the 2024 Global Game Jam. In the project, my role was to make and spawn the objects players could interact with. The objects include:

- Tomatoes

Every so often, tomatoes will get thrown towards the players. If a player gets hit by the tomatoes, the laugh meter will fill up. Every time a tomato spawns, there is chance for it to be an exploding tomato, throwing objects around on impact.

- Hammer

Every set amount of time, a swinging hammer will be lowered from the ceiling. If a player gets hit by the hammer, the laugh meter would fill.

- Blocks

Every set amount of time, the ground where players stand on will shoot up, throwing players on top and on to the side of it. If a player gets thrown, the laugh meter fills up.

Defend

Description

Defend is a project made to showcase how an AI could help players to make decisions in a tower defense game. In this project, players can press a button and ask the AI for suggestions in various aspects of the game.

Details

Made as a university project, this project shows how an AI can be used to help players in a tower defense game.

AI

In this project, players can press a button and request the AI for suggestions. The AI takes in these considerations:

- Tower Type

The AI looks for enemies in the scene, and sums up all their different stats. The AI then uses that to decide which tower type is currently the most suitable to buy.

- Platform

The AI checks if buying a platform is better than buying a tower. A platform can be placed on a block to increase its height, increasing the range of towers placed on top of it. The AI calculates the projected price of towers compared to using the platform to decide.

- Upgrades

If buying a platform isn't viable, the AI checks if buying an upgrade will. The AI takes in the needed damage type, finds the cheapest tower to upgrade. The AI checks to buy an upgrade by comparing the projected price of buying an upgrade to the price of a brand new tower.

- Tower Placement

if all other checks failed, then the AI knows to suggest to buy a new tower. The AI checks every ground block in the scene, and see which one has the best coverage if a tower is placed on top of it.
