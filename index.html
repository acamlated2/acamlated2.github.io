Projects

Project ZipZap

Description

Project ZipZap is a single-player, isometric, movement focused video game where the player uses different dash abilities to fight groups of enemies, and to traverse the environment, with the ability to chain different dashes together to cause unique interactions.

Details

This game is made as a university project with 10 other people, collaborating together as a studio to create a full, ready to release game within the time span of one year.

We decided to split the task by their aspects from the game, and assign each person to an aspect of the game. This allows each of us to fully understand an aspect of the game and reduce the time of trying to understand someone else's work for the task assigned to us.

Role - Enemies

In this project, I took the lead on designing and implementing the enemy encounters. This included:

Enemy Design

- Created variety of enemy types, each with unique behaviours.
- Defined enemy stats and abilities, while ensuring a balanced and engaging challenge for players.
- Managed and made sure that all enemies, both in visual and technical design fits within the theme of the game.

Arena Design

- Created an arena template that can easily be used to turn parts of the levels designed by the level design team to a working arena.
- Placed enemies and consumables strategically within levels to create a sense of progression and challenge.
- Considered factors like level flow, player visibility, and cover when designing enemy placements.

System Implementation

- Designed the AI of each enemy that adapts with changes of conditions in the arena. Giving the enemies interaction between each other.

Impact & Achievements

Some accomplishment I've achieved with the systems I've implemented according to our playtests:
- Increased player engagement by balancing arenas, keeping game flow, and controlling arena difficulty level.
- Improved player's retention by creating a satisfying challenge curve. Shown by player's willingness to keep trying when they failed a level multiple times.
- Created enemy types that are memorable and easily distinguishable from each other.
